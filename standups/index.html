Before we start, I have a set of questions for you.

1. Have you ever worked in a team remotely?
2. Were you a leader in the team?
3. Is it cumbersome to co-ordinate at times?
4. Is it difficult to work together with grace and efficiency?
If yes, then standups are for you!

Some common team work issues are :

  1. Co-ordination
  2. Interdependency
  3. Constructive Communication
  4. Missing deadlines due to blockers

So, what is stand-up?

  1. Stand-up is a meeting in which attendees typically participate while standing
  2. An approach of Scrum methodology in Agile development
  3. A solution to lead a team with grace and efficiency

What all you need to start practicing standups?

  1. Get a team
  2. Select a leader (Scrum Master)
  3. Assign tasks to each member
  4. Start giving "updates" regularly

In Standups, update means 3 questions :

  1. What have you done since last standup?
  2. What are you planning to do till next standup?
  3. What problems are you facing?



Each question has a significant advantage.

What have you done since last standup?

Benefits:
  1. You get to know if team members are actively working or not
  2. You get to know how much each member is contributing to the project

What are you planning to do till next standup?

Benefits:
  1. You get to know if your team members are working on a plan, not on random tasks
  2. You get a vision for forthcoming project development

What problems are you facing?

Benefits:
  1. Blockers get resolved regularly
  2. Helps in continuous bug fixing

So far, updates can be given via

  1. Regular meetups
  2. Emails
  3. IRC
  4. Slack
  5. Or any other medium to communicate

Scrum roles in standups :

  1. Scrum Master
  2. Scrum Team
  3. Product Owner

Good practices while giving updates :

  1. Inform in as much detail as possible
  2. Be specific
  3. Ask smart questions
